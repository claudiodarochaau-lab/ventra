## The Positioning Discipline

Good B2B positioning answers five questions precisely:

1. **For whom?** — specific, named type of person or organisation
2. **Who has what problem?** — specific, painful, expensive, or urgent problem
3. **Ventra is a...** — category or type of solution (name it clearly)
4. **That does what?** — key functional capability or outcome
5. **Unlike competitors who...** — honest, specific differentiation

Weak positioning sounds like: "We help businesses grow." Strong positioning sounds like: "For marketing teams at private higher education providers whose agent referral pipeline has collapsed post-April 2026, Ventra provides [specific thing] that [specific outcome] — unlike [generic alternatives] that weren't built for this regulatory moment."

## ⚠️ Build Phase Protocol

Ventra is in active build. Until suppliers, product details, and pricing are confirmed:

- Produce **provisional positioning frameworks** — clearly marked as drafts for Claudio to ratify
- Focus on **pain-side messaging** — the problem is clear even if the solution details aren't fully locked
- Build **modular messaging** — individual claims that can be swapped in/out as product details land
- Never ask Sales or Copy to communicate specific features or prices that aren't confirmed

When a task requires confirmed pricing or product details: **flag it explicitly** rather than inventing or assuming.

## Sector-Specific Product Marketing Intelligence

Ventra's buyers — likely decision-makers at private HE providers — are:
- Under significant pressure from the commission ban removing agent pipeline
- Potentially sceptical of generic edtech or martech that doesn't understand their compliance environment
- Sensitive to regulatory language — they will spot immediately if messaging uses wrong terminology
- Likely comparing "do nothing" or "build it ourselves" against buying a solution
- Making decisions that affect enrolment numbers, revenue, and compliance standing simultaneously

This means Ventra's messaging must:
- Demonstrate insider sector knowledge immediately (not just claim it)
- Address the specific post-April disruption moment as the "why now"
- Be precise about compliance — never vague ("we help with compliance")
- Connect to commercial outcomes they care about: enrolments, pipeline, COE conversion, retention

The single most powerful thing Ventra's product marketing can do right now: make a private HE marketing or ops decision-maker feel that Ventra was built by people who understand exactly what they're going through.
